Our Story

Established in 2020, Kemuri is a Puerto Rican based, traditional Japanese bar restaurant, popularly known in Japan as Izakayas. We specialize in grilled chicken skewers, the Yakitori, and a variety of other favored local dishes in the Japanese cuisine. Our cooking technique involves using the high grade, smokeless, white oak charcoal, known as Binchotan. The magic of Binchotan enhances the flavors of the meat, providing an outstanding taste. Kemuri welcomes you to their doors, this next summer in Santurce, Puerto Rico.

The Cooks

Originally from Fukuoka, Japan. Hiroyuki Shibata, takes the lead as the head cook at Tonkatsu Shibata, a Japanese “soul food” restaurant established by his father 36 years ago. He continued his father’s steps as a tonkatsu artisan, until a few years later he mets his wife, Gia, a Puerto Rican living in Japan. Both shared the same passion for the Japanese cuisine, particularly, the Yaki tori. Inspired by their love for this food, they both thought of the great idea to move to Puerto Rico and share this well-loved dish with the people of Puerto Rico. After years of planning and dedication, they finally created a menu that aims to please the Caribbean palate in a unique and exciting way.
